a collection of ransom notes

12.10.2017

Minneapolis Institute of Art

2400 3rd Ave S

Minneapolis, MN 55404

In 1876, kidnappers took Christian Ross’ 4-year-old son. A total of 22 letters were sent to Ross, demanding $20,000 for his son’s safe return. The disappearance remained unsolved for 139 years.

These letters were the first ransom notes in American history. From frantic handwriting and smudged ink to cut up magazine letters, ransom notes have evolved quite a bit over the last century. Explore our collection of ransom notes dating from the late 1800s up to the last decade.

This exhibition was drawn from the MIA’s permanent collection of graphic arts and photography, and was complemented by key loans from private collectors.
